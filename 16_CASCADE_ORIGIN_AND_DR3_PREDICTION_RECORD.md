## On thermodynamics

No prior theory derives thermodynamic irreversibility from first principles.

The laws of thermodynamics are postulates. The Second Law (entropy never decreases) is an observed regularity elevated to an axiom. Every attempt to derive it from deeper principles has failed in a specific and consistent way:

- Boltzmann's H-theorem used the Stosszahlansatz (molecular chaos assumption), which is itself time-asymmetric. Irreversibility was assumed in order to derive irreversibility. The circularity was identified immediately by Loschmidt and was never resolved.
- Statistical mechanics explains why entropy increase is overwhelmingly probable for reversible microscopic dynamics. It does not explain why it is necessary. The reversibility paradox, Poincare recurrence, and Loschmidt's objection remain open.
- Jaynes reformulated thermodynamics via information theory (MaxEnt). This is a reformulation, not a derivation from dynamics. Irreversibility is still assumed.
- Prigogine studied dissipative structures and non-equilibrium thermodynamics. Still inside thermodynamics, not below it.

Without exception, every prior derivation either postulates irreversibility at some level or derives it from assumptions that contain irreversibility in disguise.

The cascade model is the first framework to derive energy monotonicity (the thermodynamic arrow) as a mathematical theorem from discrete structural axioms. The proof does not assume irreversibility. It proves it. The forbidden Y-Y channel combined with asymmetric interaction rates makes it mathematically impossible for total energy to increase. This is not a statistical tendency. It is a structural necessity.

The Second Law equivalent in the cascade model is a theorem. Not an observation. Not a postulate. Not a statistical approximation. A proved consequence of the asymmetric structure of the interaction space.
