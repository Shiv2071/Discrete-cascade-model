## Scope

This specification defines one late-time Discrete Stochastic Cascade Dynamics
(DSCD) sector coupled to a spatially flat FLRW background governed by standard
general relativity. DSCD is the dynamical dark sector. GR supplies the
background constraint and the relation between proper time, scale factor, and
redshift.

The corrected model does not prescribe a function \(w(z)\). It evolves a
stochastic state and produces \(\rho_C\), \(p_C\), \(H\), and BAO distances from
the resulting trajectory. `CAS_LOG`, `CAS_POLY`, CPL, and constant-\(w\) models
are controls, not definitions of DSCD.

This is a background-only realization. It does not derive gravity, the sound
horizon, perturbation stability, structure growth, or a physical origin for
BAO.

## Source-dynamics audit

The compressed reference system contains the state
\((X,Y,\beta,S,S_{-1},S_{-2},\phi)\), asymmetric \(XY/XX\) channels, a forbidden
\(YY\) channel, beat-modulated interaction efficiency, irreversible structure,
and ripple-dependent regimes.

The following properties are reused only with the stated qualifications:

| Source property | Status in the cosmological system |
|---|---|
| Nonnegative resource | Re-proved by funding every event before it occurs. |
| Resource monotonicity | Pathwise for positive event costs; equality is allowed on quiet steps. |
| Exact debit identity | Re-proved from the funded budget. Post-debit clipping is prohibited. |
| Markov property | Holds only for the extended state including two structure histories and phase. |
| Asymmetry and beat | Dynamical mechanisms; their effect is established by ablation, not assumed from a fitted curve. |
| Finite activity/absorption | Not transferred to cosmology without separate hypotheses. |
| Finite total capacity | Not transferred to an expanding universe. The cosmological state is an intensive free-capacity density. |
| No-phantom result | A conditional GR/background corollary of positive depletion, not a standalone Paper I theorem. |

The historical `cascade_model.py` samples interactions before reserving all
costs and then clamps negative capacity to zero. Consequently, its nominal
debit can exceed the actual debit. It remains a compressed reference
implementation, not an invariant oracle for this realization.

## State and clock

Cells form a finite weighted comoving graph. At integration point \(j\),

\[
Z_j = \{N_j,a_j,E_j,\rho_{C,i},X_i,Y_i,S_i,S_{i-1},S_{i-2},
\phi_i,r_i\}_{i=1}^{P},
\]

where \(N=\ln a\), \(E=H/H_0\), and \(r_i\) is the regime. The transition rates
are rates per dimensionless proper time \(\tau=H_0t\). The e-fold lattice is
only an integration coordinate:

\[
d\tau = \frac{dN}{E}.
\]

The stochastic filtration is generated by the initial state and all event
draws up to the current step. Statements are labelled pathwise, ensemble, or
distributional.

## DSCD dynamics

For each cell, requested interaction events are Poisson variables with
proper-time hazards

\[
R_{XY}=\alpha_{XY}\omega_X\omega_Y\Theta(\phi)XY,\qquad
R_{XX}=\alpha_{XX}\omega_X^2 X(X-1)/2,
\]

\[
\Theta(\phi)=\vartheta+(1-\vartheta)(1+\cos\phi)/2,\qquad
\dot\phi=\omega_X-\omega_Y.
\]

Every requested event is capped by species availability and the remaining
local resource budget before it is committed. Leakage, explosive regeneration,
and bonds are funded sequentially from the same budget. Therefore, for each
path and cell,

\[
\rho_{C,i}^{j+1}=\rho_{C,i}^j-d_i^j,\qquad
0\le d_i^j\le \rho_{C,i}^j.
\]

No post-update clamp is part of the debit proof.

The ripple is a nonuniform-step approximation to proper-time structural
curvature:

\[
F_i^j =
\frac{2}{\Delta\tau_j+\Delta\tau_{j-1}}
\left|
\frac{S_i^j-S_i^{j-1}}{\Delta\tau_j}
-\frac{S_i^{j-1}-S_i^{j-2}}{\Delta\tau_{j-1}}
\right|.
\]

Regimes are quiescent for \(F\le C\), leakage for
\(C<F<C+\Delta\), and explosive for \(F\ge C+\Delta\). Structure is updated
from funded interactions and bonds. Transport uses an undirected weighted
graph Laplacian and conservative substeps.

## GR coupling

The graph-weighted mean capacity density is the DSCD dark-sector density:

\[
\bar\rho_C = \frac{\sum_i v_i\rho_{C,i}}{\sum_i v_i}.
\]

This is a defining state identity of this cosmological realization, not a
post-hoc observation map and not a claim about Paper I's extensive total
capacity.

Matter and radiation are separately conserved. The primary realization has no
inter-sector transfer \(Q\):

\[
E^2(N)=\Omega_m e^{-3N}+\Omega_r e^{-4N}
       +c_C\bar\rho_C(N).
\]

The coefficient \(c_C\) is fixed by the present flatness condition
\(E(0)=1\). DSCD generates the depletion rate
\(\mathcal D_C=-d\bar\rho_C/d\tau\). Covariant conservation defines the
background pressure:

\[
p_C=-\bar\rho_C+\frac{\mathcal D_C}{3E},\qquad
w_C=-1+\frac{\mathcal D_C}{3E\bar\rho_C}.
\]

Thus \(w_C\ge -1\) only on intervals where
\(\bar\rho_C>0\) and \(\mathcal D_C\ge0\). This is an expansion-equivalent
background result. It does not establish a microscopic stress tensor, NEC,
ghost freedom, or perturbative stability.

The exact interval diagnostic is

\[
\bar w_{j+1/2}
=-1-\frac{\ln(\bar\rho_C^{j+1}/\bar\rho_C^j)}
{3\ln(a_{j+1}/a_j)}.
\]

The likelihood consumes the simulated \(E(z)\), not this diagnostic.

## Early-time boundary

Version 1 begins at finite late-time redshift. Above the start redshift the
DSCD density is held at its initial finite value only for the purpose of
checking that its fractional contribution rapidly becomes negligible relative
to matter and radiation. If this gate fails, a fixed external sound horizon is
not allowed and the model must be extended through recombination.

BAO uses only \(\theta=H_0r_d/c\). BAO-only results may not be reported as
separate measurements of \(H_0\) or \(r_d\).

## Validation order

1. Deterministic one-cell funded accounting.
2. Tiny stochastic hazard and mechanism checks.
3. Two/three-cell conservative transport.
4. One-cell DSCD+GR analytic limits and residuals.
5. Synthetic trajectory-to-distance recovery without DESI.
6. Pilot ensemble.
7. Resolution, graph, ensemble, and identifiability gates.
8. Retrospective DR1/DR2 scoring.

Every stage emits strict JSON. A failed stage blocks all later stages.

## Version 2 forecasting layer

Version 2 answers one question: **given the DR1+DR2 history, do the DSCD+GR
realizations compatible with it converge on a finite, stable, calibrated
prediction for the DR3 tracer layout?**

### Latent prior

Eight declared dimensions sampled with scrambled Sobol: a log-uniform
depletion scale over \([10^{-1},10^{3}]\) applied to every funded cost and
the leakage rate, the interaction strengths \(\alpha_{XY}\) and
\(\alpha_{XX}\), the beat asymmetry \(\omega_Y-\omega_X\), the initial
activity and Y/X ratio, the explosive regime rate, and \(\Omega_m\). Bounds
are declared once in `dscd_forecast_prior.py` and never tuned against data.
Realizations that cannot complete a trajectory to the present receive zero
weight and are recorded with their typed failure reason.

### History compatibility

Each sample runs a fixed-seed stochastic ensemble. All compressed BAO
distances scale exactly as \(s=1/\theta\), so \(s\) is marginalized in
closed form per realization (flat prior; Gaussian conditional posterior)
and its uncertainty is propagated to predictions through Gauss-Hermite
nodes. Weights are self-normalized importance weights from the joint
DR1+DR2 marginal likelihood with per-release official covariance plus the
seed covariance. DR1/DR2 overlap is declared and bounded by a DR2-only
sensitivity.

### Gates (audit `audit_dscd_v2_forecast.py`)

- V6a: engine stress at the prior corners (exact accounting, positivity,
  non-phantom intervals, typed failures).
- G1: interval stability under Sobol halving.
- G2: interval stability across disjoint trajectory seed halves.
- G3: effective sample size, DR2-only conditioning shift, finite informative
  intervals.
- G4: end-to-end synthetic coverage calibration on prior-drawn truths run
  blind through the identical pipeline.

Identifiability of individual DSCD parameters is explicitly **not** a v2
gate: internal parameters may remain degenerate provided the forward
prediction converges. A forecast coincident with \(\Lambda\)CDM is a
falsifiable forecast, not a failure. If any gate fails the audit returns
`NO_FORECAST_V2` with the failing gate; if all pass, a versioned seal
(`18_DSCD_V2_DR3_FORECAST_RECORD.md`) is written and the historical v1
artifacts are preserved unchanged.
